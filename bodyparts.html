One of the most important things about body building is knowing what muscle groups your body has and what exercises best target them. Click on various sections of the image to see a description of the body part and what exercises best target them.

Upper Body Muscle Groups

Biceps And Forearms

Training of the bicep muscles is crucial for building huge arms. To effectivly train these muscles, focus on the concentric portion of the movement, which is where you lower the arm after curling the weight. It is recommended that use both heavy and light weights to train this muscle.

Muscles Involved
  • Bicep Brachii (long head)
  • Brachialis
  • Coracobrachialis(short head)
Best Exercies
  • Dumbbell Curls (long head emphasis)
  • Hammer Curls (Brachialis emphasis)
  • Preacher (short head emphasis)

Triceps

Contrary to popular belief, it is not the biceps that make your arms look huge, it is the triceps. In fact, the tricep muscles take up two-thirds of the muscles in your arms. If you want to make your arms look bigger, focus more on the triceps. The triceps will respond best to high intensity training. After picking your workouts for the tricep, preform them with a high number of reps.

Muscles Involved
  • Long Head
  • Medial Head
  • Lateral head
Best Exercies
  • Tricep Kickbacks (Long head emphasis)
  • Close-Grip Bench Press (Lateral Head emphasis)
  • Skullcrushers (Medial head emphasis)

Shoulders

There are three main heads of the deltoid muscle; the anterior, medial, and posterior deltoid. For most other gym goers, main focus is put on the anterior deltioid. This is because many other exercise such as the bench press or barbell curl work this muscle as a secondary muscle group worked. This often leaves the medial and posterior deltoid underdeveloped as compared to the anterior deltoid. To get those boulder shoulders, dont forget to show attention to the medial and posterior deltoids.

Muscles Involved
  • Anterior Deltoid (Front Delt)
  • Medial/Lateral Deltoid (Side Delt)
  • Posterior Deltoid (Rear Delt)
Best Exercies
  • Lateral Raises
  • Cable Face Pulls
  • Barbell Overhead Press

Chest

Any good fit physique is incomplete without the full development of the chest. Common practices to training the chest start with dividing the chest into three areas of focus. Those three areas are the upper chest, mid chest, and lower chest. Form a chest routine that will use exercises that hit all three regions of the chest. The chest muscle fibers respond best to a combination of strength focused exercies and hypertrophic exercices. For example, lifting heavy weight when preforming a flat bench press for strangth, and picking a low to moderate weight when preforming an dumbbell incline press will help your chest development.

Muscles Involved
  • Pectoralis Major
  • Pectorallis Minor
Best Exercies
  • Bench Press
  • Machine Incline Press
  • Cable Flies (Any variation)

Back

Its not enough to only good from the front, but also the back. Building your back can be difficult due to the dozens of muscles that share the back. The key to training the back is to utilize the mind-muscle connections and focus on both heavy and light weight movements. This will help turn your back into a shield.

Muscles Involved
  • Trapezius "Traps"
  • Rhomboids
  • Latissimus Dorsi "Lats"
  • Teres Major/Minor
  • Erector Spinae
Best Exercies
  • Deadlift
  • Pull-Ups
  • Bent-Over Barbell/Dumbbell Rows
  • Cable/Dumbbell Rows

Abdomen

The abdomen, or abs are the main target of development for a lot of bodybuilders. Having a six-pack shows the amount of dedication one has put in the gym. However, the abs play a very significant role as a stabalizer for every other exercie of the other muscle groups. Having a strong core can allow you to lift heavier in other lifts such as the squat or deadlift. To effectively train the abs, train by lifting a heavy load in a short, intense workout. This will help build strength resistance in your core. Keep in mind that having a strong core does not immediately result in a six-pack figure. Six-packs are more dependant on proper dieting rather than proper training.

Muscles Involved
  • Rectus abdominus
  • External Obliques
  • Internal Obliques
  • Transverse abdominus
  • Serratus anterior
Best Exercies
  • Plank
  • Mountain Climber
  • Reverse Crunch
  • Leg Raise

Lower Body Muscle Groups

Quadriceps

The quadriceps or quads emcopnass the largest musle groups of the body. They make up the muscles on the front of your thigh. To build your own tree trunks, focus on lifting heavy with these exercises. Employing proper form is key when training the quads.

Muscles Involved
  • Vastus lateralis
  • Vastus medialis
  • Vastus intermedius
  • Rectus femoris
Best Exercies
  • Squat
  • Bulgarian Split Squat
  • Leg Extensions
  • Leg Press Machine

Hamstrings and Glutes

The hamstrings and glutes make up the leg muscles on the back of your thighs. These muscles all work to help provide stabability when preforming other exercises. Compound movements and isolation exercises will help develop this muscle region of the body.

Muscles Involved
  • Gluteus maximus
  • Gluteus medius
  • Gluteus minimus
  • Semitendinosus
  • Semimembranosuss
  • Long head biceps femoris
  • Short head biceps femoris
Best Exercies
  • Romanian Deadlifts
  • Leg Curls
  • Good Mornings
  • Glute Bridges
  • Hip Thrusts

Calves

No good lower body development is complete withou strong calves. These muscles tend to be the hardest to develop for every bodybuilder due to them. The best way to train them is to train them in high frequency and employ both rep heavy and rep light exercises.

Muscles Involved
  • Gastrocnemius
  • Soleus
Best Exercies
  • Calf Raises
  • Seated Machine Calf Raises
  • Isolation Calf Raises
  • Glute Bridges
  • Hip Thrusts
